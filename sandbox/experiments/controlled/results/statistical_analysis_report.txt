================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

The controlled experiments provide empirical support for the core hypothesis:

'Multi-objective self-driven systems (MOSS) produce more sustainable,
 balanced behavior than single-objective or random strategies.'

Key evidence:
1. MOSS learns effectively (4.0 knowledge) while surviving (43 steps)
2. CuriosityOnly learns more (5.1) but dies quickly (20 steps)
3. SurvivalOnly survives longest (191 steps) but learns nothing (0.0)
4. MOSS finds the balance - sustainable autonomous learning

This validates that self-driven motivation with multiple objectives
can produce complex, adaptive behavior similar to biological systems.
